Sabatier Fuel Plant Project

Having a reliable source of fuel on Mars is essential to the development of a self-sustainable Martian base. Through the Sabatier Fuel Plant Project, the team proposes plans for a full-scale propellant production plant, based on SpaceX’s Mars mission architecture.

For Phase 1, the team builded a reactor setup to study parameters of the Sabatier Reaction. Through this process, they were able to convert carbon dioxide and hydrogen gas to form methane in Mars. As a result of this, the team was able to combine the methane and oxygen to produce the propellant to send rockets back to Earth. In Phase 2, the team planed to propose a full-scale fuel production facility and studied other areas to utilize Mars’s resources efficiently.

When Phase 2 was completed, the team verified and optimized the proposed theoretical design. They presented their research and results at the International Aeronautical Congress (IAC) in Dubai, U.A.E., 2021.
